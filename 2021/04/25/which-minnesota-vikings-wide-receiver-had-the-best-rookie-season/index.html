Which Minnesota Vikings Wide Receiver Had the Best Rookie Season?

2021-04-25

If you are as big as a Minnesota Vikings fan as I am, you would know that the Vikings have had their fair share of talented wide receivers in the past. From Hall of Famer Cris Carter to Justin Jefferson who is entering his second season in the league. For this project I wanted to look at three different Viking receivers and compare who each of their rookie seasons by looking at three main statistics: receptions, receiving yards, and touchdowns.

As you you can see, Moss had 69 receptions for a total of 1,313 recieving yards. That defintely helped him out to set that single season record for recieving touchdowns as rookie with 17 as well as set the franchise record for receiving yards in a season.

Now we’ll be look at the Vikings first round pick in the 2020 NFL draft from LSU, Justin Jefferson. Jefferson put together an impressive rookie campaign filling in the spot for Stepon Diggs who was traded to the Buffalo Bills after the 2020 season. Jefferson did not only break Moss’s franchise record for single season receiving yards with 1,400 yards but also the NFL record for rookie receieving yards previously held by Anquan Boldin.

This step chart shows us how many receiving yards Harvin had throughout his rookie year. In comparison Harvin’s receiving yards are nearly as close as Moss or Jefferson but he was very consistent. By the end of the season he only had 60 receptions and eight touchdowns, two of them being kick returns. Harvin was the first Vikings player to score a touchdown in his first three games in three different ways. Simply electric. He was as well a late addition the Pro Bowl as a kick returner.

After looking at these three Vikings receivers rookie season I think it depends on who you ask who had the better rookie season. Older fans tend to say Moss because no has seen a receiver with the skill set he had. But, if you ask a younger football fan they would say Jefferson because we simply were not alive to witness what Moss was capable of.

In my opinion, I firmly believe Harvin was the most exciting Vikings rookie to watch. Whenever he had the ball he was a threat to score, but Jefferson is the better reciever than both.
